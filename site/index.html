Reactivation ROI: what is the win-back worth?

Each reactivated customer is modelled to place one order at their own historical average order value; targets are dialled highest-priority first, so early calls carry the most value. Benchmark: email win-back campaigns typically convert 1-5%, outbound phone to known customers higher. 8% is a deliberately conservative default, not a promise.

Method & caveats

  • Data: UCI Online Retail: a real UK gift e-commerce dataset, 541,909 rows, Dec 2010 to Dec 2011.
  • Cleaning: kept rows with a known customer, positive quantity and price; cancellations (invoices starting “C”) excluded. 397,884 sale rows remain.
  • RFM: Recency, Frequency, Monetary each scored 1-4 by quartile (NTILE). Snapshot date = one day after the last invoice.
  • Dormancy threshold: a customer is a reactivation target if dormant ≥ 90 days and a top-half lifetime spender, or already flagged “At Risk (was valuable)”.
  • ROI model: one recovered order per reactivation at the customer’s historical average order value. Illustrative, not a forecast.
